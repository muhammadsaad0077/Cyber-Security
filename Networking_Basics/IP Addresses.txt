Let's say
IP Address 192.168.1.3

Subnet 255.255.255.0

Default Gateway: 192.168.1.1

Now the subnet here shows that three octets (network portion) will always remain same for every device connected within network and 4th octet will be unique.

And suppose that 3 to 4 devices are connected in a network, first device share a img and its destination ip address is 192.168.1.5, by looking at three octets your device will know that
it is the device in my network and share that information to the device.

but if the destination ip address is something 132.12.3.2 then device will go to default gateway (your router) and then router continue this further communication.

192.168.1.0 (first address, This address identifies the network itself)    192.168.1.1  (default gateway)    192.168.1.255 (broadcast Address, it will share the packets to each device on a subnet) these addresses cannot be used by any device within a network, kind of like a reserved addresses. 


IP addresses are further divided into the different classes A, B, C, D, E, 127.0.0.1 (Subnet 255.0.0.0 ) are loop back addresses, they are kind of for testing our network.
